Hiring Guide

Where to Find Baristas: Wuzzuf.net, Facebook Groups ("Specialty Coffee Egypt", "Cairo Jobs"), Instagram DMs to baristas at other cafes, SCA Training Centers, word of mouth, walk-ins with a "Now Hiring" sign.

3-Stage Evaluation

Stage 1: Phone Screen (15 min)

  • Why coffee? (passion = retention)
  • What do you drink at home?
  • Where do you live? (commute kills attendance)
  • Availability for shifts?

Stage 2: In-Person (30 min)

  • Warm and presentable?
  • Handles pressure scenarios?
  • Clear communication (AR + EN)?
  • Would you want them greeting you daily?

Stage 3: Bar Test (45 min)

  • Espresso extraction (dial in grind?)
  • Milk steaming (microfoam, no screaming)
  • Latte art (heart = pass)
  • Drink recipe knowledge
  • Cleanliness and speed
Red Flags: Cannot explain why they left previous job | Zero coffee curiosity | Dirty habits during trial | Arrogant attitude | Lives far away (60+ min commute) | Phone addiction | Freezes under pressure | Poor hygiene
SCA Certifications:
Foundation: 3,000-6,000 EGP | Intermediate: 5,000-10,000 EGP | Professional: 10,000-20,000+ EGP
Training at The Roastery (SCA-accredited, Cairo)
Retention Tips: Pay above market. Clear growth path. Performance bonuses. Respect and consistency in scheduling. Fund education (SCA certs). Provide good equipment (baristas notice).

Pricing Psychology

  • Anchor pricing: Put your most expensive drink first on the menu. Everything else feels reasonable.
  • Size upsell: Regular to Large costs you ~5 EGP more. Charge 15 EGP more. 200% markup on the upsell.
  • Add-ons: Oat milk +15, extra shot +15, syrup +10. Small amounts add up fast.
  • No discount culture: Never discount your coffee. Give a free cookie instead. Discounts train customers to wait for sales.

Highest Margin Plays

  • Cold Brew -- highest margin drink on the menu. Push it hard in summer.
  • Americano -- cheap to make, high perceived value. Staff should recommend it.
  • Tea -- extremely low cost. Good for non-coffee drinkers who come with groups.
  • Combo deals -- Latte + Croissant: 110 EGP to 95 EGP combo. Customer feels a deal, you still make ~65 EGP margin.

Verdict: Rent, Don't Buy

For a first cafe, renting is the only smart move. You need capital for equipment, inventory, and surviving months 1-18. Tying up 3-15M EGP in property means less cash for operations. Rent preserves flexibility -- if the location doesn't work, you move. Push for a 5-year lease with 1-3 months rent-free fitout period and annual increases capped at 7-10%.

Partnership Framework
You and your sister -- agree on these before spending a single pound. Review with a lawyer to make it official.
Disclaimer: This is NOT legal advice. It is a framework to guide your discussions. Hire a lawyer to formalize everything into a proper Musharakah partnership agreement under Egyptian commercial law.

1 Ownership & Capital

Ownership Split50 / 50
Your Capital Contribution (%)
Sister's Capital Contribution (%)50%
What counts as capital: Cash contributed, equipment purchased, deposits paid. Time invested is separate from capital -- it is compensated through profit share and/or management salary.
Additional capital later: If one partner contributes more capital after launch, document it. Options: (a) treat as a profit-sharing loan, (b) adjust ownership proportionally, or (c) treat as a loan with no riba -- repaid from profits before distribution. Agree on which approach upfront.

2 Roles & Responsibilities

You (Remote Partner): Financial planning, budgets, and forecasting. Systems and tech (POS setup, automation, analytics). Strategic decisions and business development. Bookkeeping oversight and P&L review.
Sister (On-Ground Partner): Daily operations and cafe management. Supplier relationships and procurement. Staff management, scheduling, and training. On-ground logistics in Cairo -- permits, maintenance, emergencies.
Decision Framework:
Day-to-day decisions under EGP -- whoever is on-ground decides.
Decisions over that amount -- both partners must agree.
Strategic decisions (new location, major hires, menu overhaul) -- both must agree, no exceptions.

3 Profit Distribution

Distribution FrequencyMonthly recommended (review quarterly)
Reinvest % (first 2 years)
Distribute %50%
Zakat: Set aside 2.5% of qualifying wealth annually. See the Zakat Calculator page for details.
After year 2: Revisit the reinvestment ratio. As the business stabilizes, you can shift to distributing more (e.g. 30% reinvest / 70% distribute).

4 What If Things Go Wrong

Disagreements: Agree on a third-party mediator before you need one. Options: trusted family member, business mentor, or Islamic arbitration (tahkeem). The mediator's decision is binding for the specific dispute.
If one partner wants out: Remaining partner gets first right to buy the departing partner's share at fair market value (assessed by an independent accountant). Minimum 6-month notice period so the business can adjust.
If the business fails: Losses are split 50/50, matching ownership. All debts are settled first. Remaining assets are divided equally. No blame, no resentment -- this is business, and you both took the risk.
If one partner cannot contribute time: Discuss openly. Options: (a) adjust profit share temporarily to reflect actual contribution, (b) hire a replacement for their role and deduct the salary from their share, or (c) keep 50/50 if both agree. Whatever you decide, document it.

5 Exit & Succession

If one partner passes away: Their share passes to heirs per Islamic inheritance law (fara'id). The surviving partner has first right of refusal to purchase the inherited share at fair market value. Important: address this in writing.
Can either partner sell to an outsider? Recommended: No. The other partner always gets first right of refusal. This is a family business -- keep it that way.
If both want to sell: Both partners must agree on the sale price. Engage an independent business valuator. Split proceeds 50/50 after settling all debts and obligations.

6 Documenting It

Formalize as a Musharakah partnership agreement -- hire a lawyer experienced in Islamic commercial law in Egypt.
Register properly with Egyptian commercial registration authority.
Keep copies in the shared Google Drive and each partner holds a physical signed copy.

⏱ Hours & Schedule

Before Iftar: Most specialty cafes close or run limited hours during fasting (sunrise to sunset). Some open for takeaway only for non-fasting customers.
After Iftar (sunset to 2-3 AM): PEAK hours. This is when Egypt comes alive. Extended evening hours are essential.
Suhoor (3-4 AM): Some cafes stay open for suhoor. Evaluate if your location has demand.
Recommended schedule: Close 2PM-6PM (dead hours during fasting), open 6PM-2AM (prime time).
Staff shifts need complete restructuring for this month.

🍽 Special Ramadan Menu

Dates + Arabic coffee -- traditional iftar starter. Must have.
Sahlab -- warm, creamy, perfect post-iftar. Your signature Sahlab Latte would shine here.
Qamar el-Din (apricot drink) -- classic Ramadan beverage.
Heavier pastries: Kunafa, Qatayef, Basbousa -- partner with a bakery or make simple versions.
Ramadan combo: Dates + Arabic coffee + mini kunafa -- special pricing.
Reduce cold drinks (less demand during cooler evening hours).
Traditional drinks: Add Tamr Hindi (tamarind) and Karkade (hibiscus) -- classic Ramadan favorites.

👥 Staffing Adjustments

Staff are fasting -- shorter active shifts, avoid heavy labor during fasting hours.
Shift structure: Split shifts or evening-only during Ramadan.
Provide iftar meal for staff working the evening shift (mandatory culturally).
Consider hiring 1 temporary part-time worker for the Ramadan rush.
Be flexible and compassionate -- fasting while working is hard.

📣 Marketing Opportunities

Ramadan decorations -- lanterns (fanous), crescent, warm lighting. Transform the space.
"Iftar at Bayt" -- position the cafe as a gathering spot for friends post-iftar.
Loyalty special: Double stamps on the loyalty card during Ramadan.
Social media: Ramadan content calendar, daily greetings, special menu highlights.
Charity collaboration: Partner with a charity for "suspended coffee" (pay-it-forward) during Ramadan. Great for brand and sawab.

💰 Financial Impact

Expect: Lower daytime revenue, significantly higher evening revenue.
Net effect: Usually neutral to slightly positive if you plan well.
Extra costs: Special menu items (dates, kunafa), decorations, temporary staff.
Revenue boost: Extended hours, higher evening traffic, group gatherings, Ramadan specials.
Eid al-Fitr (end of Ramadan): MASSIVE day. Plan a special Eid event and menu. Stock up well in advance.

How Zakat Works on Business

Rate: 2.5% of net qualifying assets after one lunar year.
Zakat IS due on: Cash in bank, inventory (beans, cups, etc. at cost value), money owed to you by customers (receivables).
Zakat is NOT due on: Equipment, furniture, fixtures -- assets used in business, not intended for trade.
