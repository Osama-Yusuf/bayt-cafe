Hiring Guide

Where to Find Baristas: Wuzzuf.net, Facebook Groups ("Specialty Coffee Egypt", "Cairo Jobs"), Instagram DMs to baristas at other cafes, SCA Training Centers, word of mouth, walk-ins with a "Now Hiring" sign.

3-Stage Evaluation

Stage 1: Phone Screen (15 min)

  • Why coffee? (passion = retention)
  • What do you drink at home?
  • Where do you live? (commute kills attendance)
  • Availability for shifts?

Stage 2: In-Person (30 min)

  • Warm and presentable?
  • Handles pressure scenarios?
  • Clear communication (AR + EN)?
  • Would you want them greeting you daily?

Stage 3: Bar Test (45 min)

  • Espresso extraction (dial in grind?)
  • Milk steaming (microfoam, no screaming)
  • Latte art (heart = pass)
  • Drink recipe knowledge
  • Cleanliness and speed
Red Flags: Cannot explain why they left previous job | Zero coffee curiosity | Dirty habits during trial | Arrogant attitude | Lives far away (60+ min commute) | Phone addiction | Freezes under pressure | Poor hygiene
SCA Certifications:
Foundation: 3,000-6,000 EGP | Intermediate: 5,000-10,000 EGP | Professional: 10,000-20,000+ EGP
Training at The Roastery (SCA-accredited, Cairo)
Retention Tips: Pay above market. Clear growth path. Performance bonuses. Respect and consistency in scheduling. Fund education (SCA certs). Provide good equipment (baristas notice).

Pricing Psychology

  • Anchor pricing: Put your most expensive drink first on the menu. Everything else feels reasonable.
  • Size upsell: Regular to Large costs you ~5 EGP more. Charge 15 EGP more. 200% markup on the upsell.
  • Add-ons: Oat milk +15, extra shot +15, syrup +10. Small amounts add up fast.
  • No discount culture: Never discount your coffee. Give a free cookie instead. Discounts train customers to wait for sales.

Highest Margin Plays

  • Cold Brew -- highest margin drink on the menu. Push it hard in summer.
  • Americano -- cheap to make, high perceived value. Staff should recommend it.
  • Tea -- extremely low cost. Good for non-coffee drinkers who come with groups.
  • Combo deals -- Latte + Croissant: 110 EGP to 95 EGP combo. Customer feels a deal, you still make ~65 EGP margin.

Verdict: Rent, Don't Buy

For a first cafe, renting is the only smart move. You need capital for equipment, inventory, and surviving months 1-18. Tying up 3-15M EGP in property means less cash for operations. Rent preserves flexibility -- if the location doesn't work, you move. Push for a 5-year lease with 1-3 months rent-free fitout period and annual increases capped at 7-10%.
