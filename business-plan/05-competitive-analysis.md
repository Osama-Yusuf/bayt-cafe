## Cilantro Deep-Dive

Cilantro is **the** Egyptian café chain. Understanding them is essential.

### What Cilantro Gets Right
- **Ubiquity** — 100+ branches means brand familiarity
- **Consistency** — Same menu, same look everywhere
- **Convenience** — Locations near offices, malls, universities
- **Safe choice** — People default to it when they don't know what else to try
- **Retail products** — They sell packaged coffee, hot chocolate, etc.

### Where Cilantro Falls Short (from Tripadvisor + Customer Feedback)

| Weakness | Customer Quote/Sentiment |
|----------|------------------------|
| **Declining quality** | "Overpriced for what you get" — common complaint |
| **Inconsistent service** | "Unprofessional staff" at multiple locations |
| **Generic atmosphere** | Every branch looks the same — corporate, not cozy |
| **Limited specialty** | No pour-over, no single origin, no cold brew (or limited) |
| **Price creep** | Prices keep rising but quality stays the same |
| **Small portions** | "Small coffee cups with tasteless flavors" |
| **Overcharging perception** | Soda at 13 EGP+ seen as unreasonable |

### How to Beat Cilantro

| Cilantro Weakness | Your Strategy |
|-------------------|---------------|
| Generic quality | Use specialty-grade beans (SCA 80+). Customers taste the difference. |
| Same boring atmosphere | Create a unique, designed space with character. |
| Poor service | Train baristas properly. Pay them above market. Happy staff = happy customers. |
| No specialty options | Offer pour-over, cold brew, single origins. Educate customers. |
| Corporate feel | Be personal. Know regulars by name. Owner-presence matters. |
| Rising prices, flat quality | Match or slightly exceed their prices, but deliver 3x the quality. |

## Customer Segments in Cairo

| Segment | Size | What They Want | Where They Go Now | How to Win Them |
|---------|------|---------------|-------------------|-----------------|
| **Young Professionals (25-35)** | Large | Good coffee, WiFi, work-friendly, Instagram-worthy | Cilantro, Costa, co-working cafés | Better coffee + better space + reliable WiFi |
| **University Students (18-24)** | Large | Affordable, social, trendy | Cilantro, generic cafés, campus spots | Student-friendly hours, loyalty card, study-friendly setup |
| **Specialty Coffee Enthusiasts** | Small but growing | Quality beans, brewing methods, latte art | Brown Nose, 30 North, The Roastery | Pour-over, single origin, transparency about sourcing |
| **Expats** | Medium (esp. Maadi) | Familiar specialty standards, English-friendly | Various, often chains for reliability | Specialty quality they'd expect in London/NYC |
| **Families** | Medium | Safe space, variety (non-coffee options for kids) | Cilantro, mall cafés | Kid-friendly menu items, welcoming atmosphere |
| **Remote Workers** | Growing | WiFi, power outlets, long-stay welcome | Co-working spaces, Cilantro | Power outlets everywhere, don't rush people out |

**Primary target: Young Professionals + Specialty Enthusiasts + Expats.** These groups overlap and represent the highest lifetime value.

## Competitive Advantages to Build

### Immediate (Day 1)

1. **Better coffee** — Specialty-grade vs commercial-grade. This alone differentiates you from 90% of Cairo cafés.
2. **Trained baristas** — Consistent, properly extracted shots. Latte art.
3. **Designed space** — Intentional, not generic. A place worth photographing.
4. **Owner presence** — You being there daily for the first year is a competitive advantage chains can't replicate.

### Medium-Term (Month 3-6)

5. **Community** — Host events, cuppings, workshops. Build a tribe.
6. **Menu innovation** — Signature drinks nobody else has.
7. **Social media voice** — Distinct, authentic, not corporate.

### Long-Term (Year 1+)

8. **Your own roast** — Roasting your own beans is the ultimate margin + quality play.
9. **Multiple locations** — If the concept works, scale it.
10. **Retail products** — Sell your beans, branded merchandise.
