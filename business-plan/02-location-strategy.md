# 02 — Location Strategy

## The Three Target Areas

### Maadi

| Factor | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Monthly Rent (80-120 sqm)** | 30,000 – 80,000 EGP |
| **Vibe** | Established, tree-lined, expat-friendly. Mix of old money and young professionals. |
| **Foot Traffic** | Moderate. Road 9 and Degla have the best walk-in traffic. |
| **Competition** | Several cafés already (Cilantro, Costa, independents). Not oversaturated yet for specialty. |
| **Target Customer** | Expats, AUC graduates, young professionals, families. |
| **Pros** | Strong community feel, loyal customer base once established, parking available, lower rent than Zamalek. |
| **Cons** | Can feel sleepy on weekday mornings. Some areas have limited visibility. |
| **Best Sub-Areas** | Degla (trendy, younger crowd), Sarayat (upscale), Road 9 corridor (high visibility). |
| **Verdict** | **Best value for money. Strong recommendation for a first café.** |

### Zamalek

| Factor | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Monthly Rent (80-120 sqm)** | 50,000 – 150,000+ EGP |
| **Vibe** | Island prestige. Cultural, artistic, cosmopolitan. |
| **Foot Traffic** | High on main streets (26th of July, Shagaret El Dor, Abou El Feda). |
| **Competition** | Dense. Many established cafés and restaurants. Cilantro, Left Bank, Sufi, etc. |
| **Target Customer** | Upper-middle class, diplomats, artists, tourists, AUC faculty. |
| **Pros** | Prestige address, high spending power, Instagram appeal, walkable neighborhood. |
| **Cons** | Very expensive rent, limited parking, intense competition, small commercial spaces, hard to find availability. |
| **Best Sub-Areas** | Shagaret El Dor street, 26th of July St, near Gezira Club. |
| **Verdict** | **High risk, high reward. Only if you have deep pockets and a strong concept.** |

### 5th Settlement (New Cairo / التجمع الخامس)

| Factor | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Monthly Rent (80-120 sqm)** | 25,000 – 70,000 EGP |
| **Vibe** | Modern, suburban, compound-life. Newer developments, younger demographic. |
| **Foot Traffic** | Car-dependent. Foot traffic concentrated in malls and strip malls. |
| **Competition** | Growing but less saturated than Maadi/Zamalek. Room for specialty. |
| **Target Customer** | Young families, university students (AUC, GUC nearby), tech workers. |
| **Pros** | Cheapest rent, growing population, underserved for specialty coffee, newer spaces (less renovation needed). |
| **Cons** | Car-dependent (no walk-in traffic unless in a mall), spread-out area, some strips feel generic. |
| **Best Sub-Areas** | Near AUC gate, Trivium Mall area, Downtown New Cairo, South Academy strip. |
| **Verdict** | **Best entry price. Ideal if targeting students/young professionals. Needs strong social media game to drive traffic.** |

## Recommendation: Where to Open First

**Maadi (Degla)** is the sweet spot for a first café:

1. **Affordable enough** to survive the learning curve (30-50K rent vs 80-150K in Zamalek)
2. **Walkable neighborhood** with existing café culture — people already go out for coffee there
3. **Loyal community** — Maadi residents support local businesses
4. **Expat presence** means customers familiar with specialty coffee and willing to pay
5. **Room for specialty** — most Maadi cafés are generic; a true specialty shop would stand out

**Second choice: 5th Settlement** — if you want the cheapest rent and are targeting university crowds. But you'll need to work harder on marketing since foot traffic is lower.

**Zamalek: later.** Open there as location #2 or #3 once you've proven the concept and have cash flow.

## Buy vs Rent vs Buy-to-Rent

### Option A: Rent the Café Space (RECOMMENDED)

| Pros | Cons |
|------|------|
| Low upfront cost (2-3 months deposit) | No equity building |
| Flexibility to relocate if it doesn't work | Rent increases annually (7-15%) |
| Preserve capital for operations | Landlord has power |
| Can negotiate fitout contributions | |

**Total upfront for rent:** 60,000 – 450,000 EGP (deposit only)

### Option B: Buy the Commercial Property

| Pros | Cons |
|------|------|
| No monthly rent payment | Massive capital tied up: 2M–15M EGP for commercial space |
| Property appreciates (strong inflation hedge in Egypt) | If business fails, stuck with property to sell |
| Full control over modifications | Opportunity cost — that capital could fund operations for years |
| Tax deductions (depreciation) | |

**Total upfront for buying:** 2,000,000 – 15,000,000 EGP depending on area

### The Clear Winner: Rent

For a **first-time café owner**, renting is the only sensible choice:

1. **Capital preservation** — You need every pound for equipment, inventory, marketing, and surviving the first 12-18 months
2. **Flexibility** — If the location doesn't work, you can move after the lease term
3. **Focus** — Managing a property AND a new business is a distraction
4. **Risk management** — Most cafés are not profitable until month 6-18. Don't add a property purchase to that stress

## What to Look for in a Space

### Must-Haves
- [ ] **Street-level** — Basement or upper floors kill foot traffic
- [ ] **Visible from the street** — Signage potential, large windows
- [ ] **80-120 sqm minimum** — Enough for 20-30 seats + bar + small kitchen
- [ ] **Water supply + drainage** — For espresso machine, dishwashing
- [ ] **Adequate electrical capacity** — Espresso machine + grinder + AC draws serious power (check 3-phase availability)
- [ ] **Ventilation / exhaust option** — If you plan to serve food
- [ ] **Restroom** — Or ability to build one
- [ ] **AC-ready** — Existing AC or the ability to install split units

### Nice-to-Haves
- [ ] Outdoor seating potential (sidewalk or terrace)
- [ ] Parking nearby
- [ ] High ceilings (feels spacious, allows industrial design)
- [ ] Corner location (visibility from two streets)
- [ ] Near complementary businesses (gym, bookstore, coworking)

### Red Flags
- Basement locations
- No water/drainage infrastructure
- Very narrow or awkwardly shaped spaces
- Landlords who won't allow fitout modifications
- Short lease terms (less than 3 years) without renewal option
- Areas with no pedestrian traffic at all

## Lease Negotiation Tips for Egypt

1. **Push for a 5-year lease** with option to renew — gives you stability and time to build the business
2. **Negotiate a fitout period** — 1-3 months rent-free while you renovate
3. **Cap annual rent increases** — Try to lock in 7-10% max annual increase (some landlords push for 15%+)
4. **Get the landlord to contribute to fitout** if the space needs major work — deducted from future rent
5. **Ensure the lease allows for café operation** specifically (some commercial leases restrict food/beverage)
6. **Include a force majeure clause** — Learned from COVID, this protects you if forced to close
7. **Right of first refusal** if the landlord sells the property
8. **Document everything** with photos before taking possession
9. **Use a lawyer** — 10-15K EGP for lease review is money well spent
