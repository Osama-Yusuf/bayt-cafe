# 03 — Products & Menu

## Philosophy: Start Small, Execute Perfectly

> "A smaller, focused menu is often better than a large, overwhelming one. Cutting slow sellers simplifies operations, reduces waste, and makes training easier."

Start with 12-15 drinks max. Master them. Add items based on customer demand, not guesswork.

## What NOT to Launch With

- Full breakfast menu (eggs, pancakes, etc.) — needs kitchen staff
- Smoothies and frappés — require blender, ice, more ingredients
- Specialty pour-over (V60, Chemex) — add in Phase 2 when baristas are trained
- Alternative milks beyond oat — add based on demand
- Pastries baked in-house — start with sourced, switch to in-house when proven

## Phased Product Rollout

### Phase 1 — Opening Day (Month 0)

**Focus: Get the coffee right.**

- Core espresso menu (espresso, americano, latte, cappuccino, flat white)
- Turkish coffee
- Cold brew + iced versions
- Tea selection
- Hot chocolate
- Fresh OJ + lemonade
- 3-4 sourced bakery items
- **Total: ~15 items**

### Phase 2 — Month 2-3 (After Stabilizing Operations)

**Focus: Expand drinks, test food.**

- Add pour-over / V60 (single origin showcase)
- Add signature drink #1 (your unique creation — see doc 08)
- Add almond milk option
- Add matcha latte
- Expand food: add 2-3 sandwiches (sourced or simple prep)
- Add seasonal special (iced in summer, spiced in winter)
- **Total: ~20 items**

### Phase 3 — Month 4-6 (Building Identity)

**Focus: Signature items and differentiation.**

- Add signature drink #2 and #3
- Add Chemex / batch brew option
- Introduce pastry baking in-house (start with 1-2 items — cookies or banana bread)
- Add smoothies / frappé-style blended drinks
- Weekend brunch special (simple: eggs + toast + coffee)
- **Total: ~25-28 items**

### Phase 4 — Month 6-12 (Full Menu)

**Focus: Become a destination.**

- Full food menu (light meals, salads, sandwiches)
- Seasonal drink rotations (quarterly)
- Retail coffee bags (sell your beans)
- Brewing equipment for sale (V60, cups, etc.)
- Catering menu for corporate orders
- **Total: ~30-35 items**

## Bakery Strategy: Source First, Bake Later

### Why Start with External Sourcing

| Factor | Source Externally | Bake In-House |
|--------|-----------------|---------------|
| Startup cost | Low (no oven, no baker) | High (+50,000-150,000 EGP equipment + baker salary) |
| Quality control | Depends on partner | Full control |
| Freshness | Daily delivery = fresh enough | Made that morning |
| Menu flexibility | Limited to what they make | Unlimited |
| Margins | Lower (you're buying at wholesale) | Higher (raw ingredients are cheap) |

**Recommendation:** Source from a local bakery for months 1-6. Build the relationship. Once you see which items sell, invest in a small oven and hire/train someone to bake your top 3 sellers in-house.

### Bakery Partners to Approach in Cairo

Look for small artisan bakeries (not factory brands) that do wholesale:
- Breadfast (delivery platform, also wholesales)
- Local neighborhood bakeries in Maadi/Zamalek
- Home bakers (growing trend in Cairo — find them on Instagram)
- Negotiate daily delivery of 20-30 pieces, with return policy for unsold items

## Coffee Bean Quality Tiers

| Tier | Quality | Cost/kg | Who Uses It | Your Position |
|------|---------|---------|-------------|--------------|
| **Commercial Grade** | Commodity, dark roast | 300-500 EGP | Cilantro, Costa, generic cafés | Not this |
| **Specialty Grade (80-84)** | Good, clean flavors | 800-1,200 EGP | Mid-range specialty cafés | **Start here** |
| **Premium Specialty (85+)** | Exceptional, traceable | 1,200-2,000 EGP | High-end specialty (Brown Nose, 30 North) | Aspire to this |
| **Competition Grade (87+)** | Top 1%, micro-lots | 2,000+ EGP | Competition, special releases | Occasional feature |

**Strategy:** Start with 80-84 score beans for your house blend (good quality, manageable cost) and offer one 85+ single origin for pour-over/filter. As you grow, shift your blend quality upward.
