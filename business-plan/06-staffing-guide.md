## Phased Hiring Plan

### Pre-Opening (Month -2 to 0)

```
YOU + SISTER (full-time, managing everything together)
├── Barista #1 (hire 2-3 weeks before opening, train during soft opening)
├── Barista #2 (hire 2-3 weeks before opening)
└── Cleaner (hire 1 week before opening)

Total staff: 3 + you two
Monthly labor: ~16,000-23,000 EGP
```

### Phase 1: Stabilization (Month 1-3)

```
YOU + SISTER (still full-time, filling every gap)
├── Barista #1 — AM shift (7am-3pm)
├── Barista #2 — PM shift (2pm-10pm)
├── Barista #3 — hired Month 2 (covers days off, peak overlap)
├── Cleaner — full day
└── Freelance Social Media — starts Month 2

Monthly labor: ~25,000-35,000 EGP
```

### Phase 2: Growth (Month 4-6)

```
YOU + SISTER (can start alternating days off)
├── Shift Lead (promoted from Barista #1 or #2, raise to 8,000-10,000)
├── Barista #2
├── Barista #3
├── Cleaner
├── Part-time Social Media
└── Part-time Accountant

Monthly labor: ~35,000-50,000 EGP
```

### Phase 3: Maturation (Month 6-12)

```
YOU (strategic oversight, 5 days/week)
├── Shift Lead
├── Barista #2
├── Barista #3
├── Barista #4 (if traffic justifies)
├── Kitchen Helper (if food menu expands)
├── Cleaner
├── Part-time Social Media
└── Part-time Accountant

Monthly labor: ~45,000-70,000 EGP
```

## How to Hire Great Baristas

### Where to Find Them

| Channel | Notes |
|---------|-------|
| **Wuzzuf.net** | Egypt's largest job board. Post under "Food & Beverage." |
| **Facebook Groups** | "Specialty Coffee Egypt", "Cairo Jobs", "وظائف باريستا مصر" |
| **Instagram** | DM baristas at other cafés who seem passionate (ethical poaching is normal) |
| **SCA Training Centers** | The Roastery and other SCA-accredited spots in Cairo — ask if graduates are looking for work |
| **Word of Mouth** | Tell your coffee-loving friends you're hiring. Best baristas come through referrals. |
| **Walk-ins** | Post a "Now Hiring" sign in your window. |

### The 3-Stage Evaluation Process

#### Stage 1: Phone Screen (15 minutes)

Ask:
1. "Why do you want to work in coffee?" — Passion vs just needing any job. Both OK, but passion = longer retention.
2. "What coffee do you drink? How do you make it at home?" — Reveals genuine interest.
3. "Where do you live? How would you commute?" — If they live in 6th of October and your café is in Maadi, attendance will be a problem. Cairo commutes are real.
4. "What's your availability?" — Need flexibility for shifts.
5. Previous experience — Helpful but not required.

**Pass/fail:** Enthusiasm + reliable transportation + availability.

#### Stage 2: In-Person Interview (30 minutes)

Assess:
- **Warmth** — Would you want this person greeting you every morning?
- **Presentation** — Clean, neat, professional without being stiff
- **Communication** — Clear Arabic, functional English (important for expat-heavy areas)
- **Composure** — Can they handle pressure? Scenario test:

Scenario questions:
- *"A customer says their latte tastes burnt. What do you do?"*
- *"It's a rush. Three orders in queue. Walk me through your process."*
- *"Someone asks for a drink not on the menu. How do you handle it?"*

**Pass/fail:** Likable + composed + good communicator.

#### Stage 3: Bar Test (30-45 minutes)

For **experienced** candidates — have them demonstrate:

| Skill | What to Watch |
|-------|--------------|
| **Espresso extraction** | Do they check the grind? Purge the group head? Time the shot? Know what 25-30 seconds means? |
| **Milk steaming** | Microfoam (tiny bubbles, not big ones). Correct temperature (55-65C). No screaming. Wipe the wand after. |
| **Latte art** | Centered heart = acceptable. Tulip = good. Rosetta = excellent. |
| **Drink building** | Can they make a flat white, cortado, cappuccino without instructions? |
| **Cleanliness** | Do they wipe the portafilter? Clean their station? Or leave a mess? |
| **Speed** | Can they make 2-3 drinks in sequence smoothly? |
| **Workflow** | Do they multitask (steaming milk while pulling a shot) or do things serially? |

For **inexperienced** candidates (willing to train):
- Show them how to tamp, pull a shot, steam milk
- Watch: Do they listen? Are their hands steady? Do they seem to enjoy it?
- **Attitude and trainability > current skill**

### Red Flags

| Red Flag | Why It Matters |
|----------|---------------|
| Can't explain why they left their last job | Drama or unreliability follows |
| Zero interest in coffee | They'll leave for the next slightly-higher-paying job |
| Dirty bar habits | Won't keep your equipment or workspace clean |
| Arrogant about their skills | Can't be coached, won't improve |
| Lives very far away | Cairo traffic = guaranteed attendance problems |
| Checks phone during trial | Will be worse when hired |
| Freezes under multitasking | Can't handle a rush |
| Poor hygiene | Customers notice immediately |

### On Top of Base Salary

| Benefit | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **Tips** | 1,000–3,000 EGP/month per barista in a busy location. Pool tips among FOH staff. |
| **Social Insurance** | Legally required. Employer: ~18.75%, Employee: ~11% of insured salary. |
| **Meals** | Free shift meal + unlimited coffee. Expected, costs you very little. |
| **Transport Allowance** | 200–500 EGP/month. Standard in Cairo given commute costs. |
| **Annual Raise** | Budget 10-20% annually (inflation in Egypt makes this necessary). |
| **Eid Bonus** | Culturally expected. Half to one month salary for Eid al-Adha. Smaller for Eid al-Fitr. |
| **Ramadan** | Shorter hours. Schedule adjustment. Some cafés close during fasting hours. |

## Training Program

### Week 1: Foundation (Before Opening)

| Day | Focus |
|-----|-------|
| Day 1-2 | Coffee theory: bean to cup, extraction basics, what makes specialty "specialty" |
| Day 3-4 | Machine operation: espresso machine, grinder, maintenance, cleaning protocols |
| Day 5 | Milk steaming: technique, temperature, texturing, latte art basics |
| Day 6 | Full menu: every drink recipe, build order, sizes, modifications |
| Day 7 | Service: POS training, greeting, upselling, handling complaints |

### Ongoing Training

| Frequency | Activity |
|-----------|---------|
| Weekly | 30-minute cupping session with team (taste different origins, discuss flavor notes) |
| Monthly | "Coffee class" — teach about origins, processing, roast profiles |
| Daily | 15 minutes before opening for latte art practice |
| Quarterly | Send a barista to an SCA course or workshop |
| As needed | Cross-training: every FOH person should do register, bar, and food |

### SCA Certifications (Investment in Staff)

| Level | Cost in Cairo | When |
|-------|-------------|------|
| Foundation (Barista Skills) | 3,000–6,000 EGP | After 3-month probation for promising baristas |
| Intermediate | 5,000–10,000 EGP | After 6-12 months for shift leads |
| Professional | 10,000–20,000+ EGP | For head barista / trainer role |

**Training centers in Cairo:** The Roastery (SCA-accredited), various SCA Authorized Trainers.

Paying for SCA certification is a powerful retention tool — it shows investment in their career.

## Retention: How to Keep Good People

Cairo's specialty café scene is booming. Good baristas get poached. Here's how to keep them:

1. **Pay above market** — Even 500-1,000 EGP above going rate dramatically reduces turnover. Replacing and retraining costs far more than the raise.

2. **Clear growth path** — "In 6 months, if you hit X benchmarks, you become Senior Barista with a 2,000 EGP raise." Written. Objective. Transparent.

3. **Performance bonuses** — Tie a small bonus (500-1,000 EGP) to monthly revenue targets. Creates ownership mentality.

4. **Respect and dignity** — F&B workers in Egypt are sometimes treated poorly. Create a culture where baristas are professionals. This is the #1 retention driver.

5. **Consistent scheduling** — Post schedules 2 weeks in advance. Respect days off. Don't call people in on their rest day unless it's an emergency.

6. **Education** — Baristas who learn and grow stay. The ones who stagnate leave.

7. **Good equipment** — Talented baristas want to work on good machines. A La Marzocco attracts better talent than a cheap machine.

8. **Small perks** — 50-100% discount on drinks, 25-50% off food, birthday acknowledgment, team outings. Low cost, high impact.

9. **Exit interviews** — When someone leaves, ask why honestly. Patterns reveal systemic issues.

10. **Don't use non-competes** — Largely unenforceable in Egypt at this level. Retention comes from culture, not legal threats.

## Labor Cost as % of Revenue

**Target: 25-35% of revenue goes to labor** (including your own draw).

| Monthly Revenue | Max Labor Budget (35%) | What That Buys |
|----------------|----------------------|----------------|
| 80,000 EGP | 28,000 EGP | 2 baristas + cleaner (tight but doable) |
| 120,000 EGP | 42,000 EGP | 3 baristas + cleaner + part-time social |
| 200,000 EGP | 70,000 EGP | Full team: shift lead + 3 baristas + kitchen + cleaner |

If labor exceeds 40% of revenue, you're either overstaffed or underpriced. Audit both.
