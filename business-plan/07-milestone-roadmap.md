# 07 — Milestone Roadmap (Dynamic Triggers)

Every phase advances based on **real signals**, not calendar dates. No phase has a fixed timeline — you move forward when the triggers are met.

---

## Phase 0: Preparation

**Duration:** 2-3 months before opening

### What You're Doing

```
PREPARATION PHASE
├── Location secured (lease signed)
├── Renovation / fitout underway
├── Equipment ordered (4-8 week lead time)
├── Branding complete (logo, menu design, signage, packaging)
├── Licenses filed and progressing
├── Instagram + TikTok accounts live, posting behind-the-scenes
├── 2 baristas hired and training during fitout
├── Cleaner hired
├── Supplier relationships confirmed (roaster, bakery, milk)
├── Soft opening (friends & family, 2-3 days before grand opening)
└── Grand opening event planned
```

### Exit Trigger → Move to Phase 1
- [ ] Space is fully built out and inspected
- [ ] All licenses obtained
- [ ] Equipment installed, tested, and dialed in
- [ ] Staff trained and can execute full menu independently
- [ ] Initial inventory stocked
- [ ] POS system live and tested
- [ ] Soft opening completed, critical feedback addressed

---

## Phase 1: Launch — "Get the Coffee Right"

### Focus
Survival and consistency. Nothing fancy — just perfect coffee, warm service, clean space, every single day.

### Products
- Core espresso menu (espresso, americano, latte, cappuccino, flat white)
- Turkish coffee
- Hot chocolate
- Tea selection (mint, chamomile, English breakfast)
- Cold brew + iced latte + iced americano
- Fresh OJ + lemonade
- 3-4 sourced bakery items (croissant, cookie, cake slice)
- **Total: ~15 items**

### Experience
- Beautiful, photo-ready space
- Consistent drink quality
- Personal service — learn every regular's name
- Good playlist, clean restrooms, reliable WiFi

### Operations
- Owner/sister present every day
- Tight opening/closing procedures
- Daily inventory tracking
- Customer feedback collection (simple form or notebook)

### Marketing
- Grand opening event
- Daily Instagram content
- Google Maps listing with photos
- Encourage reviews from early customers

### Exit Triggers → Move to Phase 2
All of these must be true simultaneously:
- [ ] **Consistent daily traffic:** 40+ customers/day for 2+ consecutive weeks
- [ ] **Drink quality stable:** No complaints about consistency for 2+ weeks
- [ ] **Operations smooth:** No major operational issues (equipment, supply, staffing) for 2+ weeks
- [ ] **Staff independent:** Baristas can run a full shift without founders intervention
- [ ] **Cash flow tracked:** You know your daily revenue, COGS, and margin numbers
- [ ] **Customer feedback positive:** 4.5+ stars on Google, regulars emerging

**Earliest realistic timing: 2-4 months.** Some cafés take 6 months to stabilize. Don't rush this.

---

## Phase 2: Stabilize — "Build the Habit"

### Focus
Turn first-time visitors into regulars. Smooth out operations. Start understanding your numbers deeply.

### Product Additions (only when Phase 1 drinks are consistently excellent)
- Pour-over / V60 (single origin showcase)
- Matcha latte
- 1st signature drink (your unique creation)
- Almond milk option
- 2 additional food items (sandwiches)
- **Total: ~20 items**

### Experience Additions
- Loyalty card launched (simple stamp card)
- Power outlets marked at every seat
- Reading corner (books, magazines)
- Baristas doing basic latte art consistently

### Operations
- 3rd barista hired (when trigger met)
- Part-time social media person (when trigger met)
- Weekly cupping sessions with staff begin
- Inventory system refined — track waste and best sellers
- Reorder triggers established for all supplies

### Marketing
- Instagram 5x/week, TikTok 3x/week
- First influencer collaboration
- "Coffee of the week" social series
- Start building email list

### Hire 3rd Barista When:
- [ ] Days off create understaffing problems
- [ ] OR daily transactions exceed 80
- [ ] OR queue times exceed 5 minutes during peak

### Hire Social Media Help When:
- [ ] You're consistently posting less than 4x/week
- [ ] OR engagement is dropping because content quality suffers
- [ ] OR you're spending 10+ hours/week on social media

### Exit Triggers → Move to Phase 3
- [ ] **Repeat customer rate:** 25-35% of daily traffic are return visitors
- [ ] **Daily traffic:** 60+ customers/day sustained for 3+ weeks
- [ ] **Revenue trend:** Month-over-month revenue growing
- [ ] **Ops stability:** No major fire-drills for 4+ weeks
- [ ] **Staff trained:** 3rd barista fully independent
- [ ] **Instagram:** 1,000+ followers with genuine engagement
- [ ] **Google reviews:** 30+ reviews, 4.5+ stars

**Earliest realistic timing: 6-9 months from opening.** This phase should NOT be rushed.

---

## Phase 3: Differentiate — "Why Us and Nobody Else"

### Focus
Build your identity. Create reasons for people to choose you specifically, not just "a café nearby."

### Product Additions (only when demand signals are clear)
- 2nd and 3rd signature drinks
- Seasonal special (summer iced / winter spiced)
- Start baking 1-2 items in-house (test with your best-selling sourced item first)
- Smoothies / frappés (only if customers ask for them)
- **Total: ~25-28 items**

### Experience Additions
- First public event (cupping, tasting, or workshop — free, 15-20 people)
- Local art on walls (artist collaboration, display + sell on commission)
- "Barista's choice" daily recommendation
- Outdoor seating (if space allows)
- Board games / books refined

### Operations
- Promote best barista to Shift Lead (with raise + defined responsibilities)
- Owner/sister can each take 1 day off/week
- Monthly P&L review established
- Part-time accountant starts
- Staff schedule posted 2 weeks in advance consistently

### Marketing
- First branded event (latte art throwdown, coffee workshop)
- Local business partnership (gym, bookstore, co-working — cross-promote)
- Press outreach (Egyptian Streets, Cairo Scene, food bloggers)
- Branded takeaway cups launched

### Bake In-House When:
- [ ] Your top-selling bakery item sells 20+/day consistently
- [ ] AND you've identified a baker or can train someone
- [ ] AND the margin improvement justifies the equipment cost

### Promote Shift Lead When:
- [ ] One barista clearly outperforms others in quality + reliability
- [ ] AND they've been with you 3+ months
- [ ] AND they can open/close independently

### Exit Triggers → Move to Phase 4
- [ ] **Daily traffic:** 80+ customers/day sustained
- [ ] **Signature drinks:** At least one signature drink in your top 5 sellers
- [ ] **Community forming:** Regulars know each other, events have 10+ attendees
- [ ] **Revenue:** Approaching or at break-even
- [ ] **Brand identity:** People recommend you by name, not just "that café on Street X"
- [ ] **Press/social proof:** At least 1 press/blog feature

**Earliest realistic timing: 10-14 months from opening.**

---

## Phase 4: Profitability — "Sustainable Business"

### Focus
Make money consistently. Optimize everything. Build systems that work without you being there every second.

### Product Additions (data-driven — add what customers want)
- Full food menu (light meals, salads, upgraded sandwiches)
- Retail coffee bags (sell your beans, branded 250g bags)
- Quarterly seasonal menu rotation
- Weekend brunch set (coffee + food combo)
- **Total: ~30-35 items**

### Experience Additions
- Monthly events calendar (cupping, workshop, community event)
- Loyalty program upgraded to digital (Bonat or similar)
- Seasonal décor refreshes
- Curated playlists by time of day

### Operations
- 4th barista if traffic justifies
- Kitchen helper if food revenue exceeds 30% of total
- Inventory management automated
- Supplier terms renegotiated (volume = leverage)
- All SOPs documented

### Marketing
- Monthly content calendar planned ahead
- Influencer partnerships (2-3/quarter)
- Local SEO optimized
- Catering menu launched (corporate coffee delivery)

### Add Food Menu When:
- [ ] Customers frequently ask "do you have food?"
- [ ] AND pastry sales are strong (validates food demand)
- [ ] AND you can hire a kitchen helper without labor cost exceeding 35% of revenue

### Launch Retail Beans When:
- [ ] Customers ask to buy beans (natural demand)
- [ ] AND your bean supplier can do custom-branded bags
- [ ] AND minimum order quantity is economically viable

### Exit Triggers → Move to Phase 5
- [ ] **Profitable:** 3+ consecutive months of net profit
- [ ] **Daily traffic:** 100+ customers/day
- [ ] **Average ticket:** 85+ EGP
- [ ] **Systems stable:** Café runs smoothly when owner is absent for 2+ days
- [ ] **Team retained:** No unplanned staff departures in 3+ months
- [ ] **Brand strong:** 5,000+ Instagram followers, recognized as top café in area

**Earliest realistic timing: 16-22 months from opening.**

---

## Phase 5: Growth — "Scale What Works"

### Focus
Prepare for expansion. Build the brand beyond one location.

### Product Additions
- Brewing equipment retail (V60, Chemex, cups)
- Branded merchandise (mugs, tote bags)
- Cold brew bottles (grab-and-go)
- Begin exploring in-house roasting

### Experience Additions
- Paid workshops (SCA-style, 200-500 EGP/ticket)
- Live music nights
- "Coffee & ..." collaboration events (art, books, film)
- Space refresh (new elements, updated décor)

### Operations
- Send top barista to SCA Intermediate certification
- Financial forecasting for Year 2
- Scout locations for café #2
- Formalize employee handbook + onboarding

### Marketing
- Brand ambassador program
- Delivery app presence (Talabat, Elmenus)
- Year-in-review storytelling content

### Scout Location #2 When:
- [ ] 6+ months consecutive profitability
- [ ] AND daily revenue supports the cost of a manager hire
- [ ] AND your operational systems are documented and transferable
- [ ] AND you've identified a neighborhood that doesn't cannibalize location #1

### Exit Triggers → Move to Phase 6
- [ ] **Sustained profitability:** 6+ months
- [ ] **Location #2 identified and lease-ready**
- [ ] **Manager candidate identified or hired**
- [ ] **Brand has recognition beyond immediate neighborhood**

**Earliest realistic timing: 22-30 months from opening.**

---

## Phase 6: Expansion — "Build the Empire"

### Focus
Multi-location operation. Brand becomes bigger than any single café.

### Key Moves
- Open café #2 (different neighborhood)
- Hire Café Manager (you can't run 2 alone)
- Own-brand roasted coffee (partner with roaster or buy a small roaster)
- Wholesale beans to other cafés
- Subscription box (monthly beans to customers)
- Central purchasing across locations
- Barista competition sponsorship

### Scale When:
- [ ] Location #1 profitable without your daily presence
- [ ] AND you have a trusted manager
- [ ] AND capital reserves support 6-12 months of location #2 losses
- [ ] AND brand reputation justifies a second location

---

## Decision Framework: "Should I Move to the Next Phase?"

Before advancing any phase, run this checklist:

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                 PHASE ADVANCEMENT CHECK                  │
│                                                         │
│  □ All exit triggers for current phase are met?         │
│  □ Met for 2+ consecutive weeks (not just one good day)?│
│  □ Team can handle current load without stress?         │
│  □ Cash flow supports the next phase's investments?     │
│  □ You've validated demand (customers asking for it)?   │
│  □ Adding complexity won't break what already works?    │
│                                                         │
│  If ANY answer is NO → Stay in current phase.           │
│  If ALL answers are YES → Advance.                      │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

### Anti-Patterns to Avoid

| Mistake | Why It Hurts |
|---------|-------------|
| Adding menu items nobody asked for | Increases waste, complexity, training burden |
| Hiring ahead of demand | Burns cash without revenue to support it |
| Hosting events before café is stable | Diverts attention from core quality |
| Expanding to location #2 while location #1 is shaky | Multiplies problems instead of profits |
| Scaling because of ego instead of data | "We should be bigger" ≠ "Customers want us bigger" |
| Comparing your Month 3 to someone else's Year 3 | Every café grows at its own pace |
